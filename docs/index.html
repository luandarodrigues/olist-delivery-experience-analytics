Core thesis

Customer dissatisfaction is not only a product issue. It is an experience chain issue: seller, freight, distance, delivery promise and post-purchase review.

Raw → clean analytical mart

The raw tables are relational. The dashboard story starts by turning them into an order-level mart.

SQL transformation logic

WITH item_agg AS (...), payment_agg AS (...), review_agg AS (...) SELECT orders.order_id, customer_state, delivery_days, delay_days, delayed_flag, items_count, sellers_count, freight_value, payment_value, review_score, CASE WHEN review_score <= 2 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END AS low_review_flag FROM orders LEFT JOIN customers LEFT JOIN item_agg LEFT JOIN payment_agg LEFT JOIN review_agg;

Final conclusion

This project shows that customer reviews in e-commerce are not only a satisfaction metric. They are a delayed signal of operational quality.

Storytelling conclusion

The strongest business opportunity is to move from reactive review analysis to proactive experience risk monitoring: identify orders likely to disappoint before the review is written.
