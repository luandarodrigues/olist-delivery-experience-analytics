Executive story

Olist’s marketplace experience is not only a sales story. The strongest operational narrative is about expectation management: when delivery promises break, reviews deteriorate.

Raw → clean analytical mart

The raw Olist tables are joined into an order-level mart. This is the modeling grain used for business insights and predictive analysis.

Raw sourceRole in martRows loaded

Transformation logic

One order can have multiple items, sellers and payments. The clean mart aggregates these many-to-one relationships before joining reviews and delivery events.

Clean grain

One row per order: delivery timing, review score, freight, item count, seller complexity, category mix and payment behavior.

Promise gap: delivery estimate vs reality

Delivery delay is measured as delivered date minus estimated delivery date. Positive values mean the promise was broken.

Predictive layer

The repository includes a Python/Pandas/Scikit-learn script to train models from raw CSVs and export metrics. The live dashboard also calculates an interpretable risk score.

Model interpretation

Accuracy alone is weak for dissatisfaction because low reviews are imbalanced. Precision, recall, ROC-AUC and business usefulness matter more.

Operational use

Use prediction as an early-warning layer: prioritize orders where delay, long route, category friction and seller complexity overlap.
