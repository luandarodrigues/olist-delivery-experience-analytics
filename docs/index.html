Raw → clean analytical mart

The raw Olist CSVs are stored in data/raw/ with Git LFS. The dashboard uses derived analytical CSVs for speed and reproducibility.

SQL transformation logic

WITH item_agg AS (...), payment_agg AS (...), review_agg AS (...) SELECT orders.order_id, customer_state, delivery_days, delay_days, delayed_flag, items_count, sellers_count, freight_value, payment_value, review_score, CASE WHEN review_score <= 2 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END AS low_review_flag FROM orders LEFT JOIN customers LEFT JOIN item_agg LEFT JOIN payment_agg LEFT JOIN review_agg;
Why this matters

The analytical mart turns raw transactional tables into one business unit of analysis: the order experience.

Delay and review risk

Delay is treated as a business assumption and tested against low-review exposure.

Monthly proof

Months with higher delay rates tend to show weaker review performance, especially in friction periods.

Same-state vs cross-state

Geography changes the operating model. Cross-state orders carry higher time and freight exposure.

Final conclusion

Customer reviews are a delayed signal of operational quality. The best analytics use is not only reporting what went wrong, but identifying where friction is likely to happen again.

Storytelling conclusion

The business opportunity is to shift from reactive review monitoring to proactive experience risk management.
